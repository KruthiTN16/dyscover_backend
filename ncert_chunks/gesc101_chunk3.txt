Like humans and animals such plantsdepend on the food produced by other plants. They use the heterotrophic mode of nutrition. Look at Fig. 1.5. Do you see a yellow wiry branched structure twining around the stem and branches of a tree? This is a plant called Cuscuta(Amarbel). It does not have chlorophyll. It takes readymade food from the plant on which it is climbing. The plant onwhich it climbs is called the host. Can you guess why algae are green in colour? They contain chlorophyll which gives themthe green colour. Algae can also prepare their own food by photosynthesis.
